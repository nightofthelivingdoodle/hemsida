And the Winner Is...

In a future where humans are gone, artificial intelligence decides what beauty means. Beneath a crumbling railway bridge, before three mysterious gates, unfolds The Most Beautiful Mixed Animal Contest. The Speakerbot — an AI with a giant mouth — is about to announce: “And the winner is...”

On the left: a giran — a swan body with a giraffe’s neck and head — stretches its elegant neck toward the light of an escalator rising endlessly into the sky. In the middle: a powerful fishire — with a fish head and horse body — gallops in from a palm-fringed beach, while a folded umbrella lies before it, trying to calm its natural skittishness. On the right: a massive mammorilla — half mammoth, half gorilla — steps out from the intersection of Prince Street and Fifth Avenue in New York. In the foreground, red poppies bloom. A praying mantis, an armadillo, and a bat hanging from the bridge’s underside are all present. On the bridge: a rusty old car with its hood open shelters a sleeping fox. Nearby stand three spectators — a squirrel, a raccoon, and a badger — who seem to be talking among themselves, clearly puzzled by the scene. Megaphones hang from the bridge, peeling plaster reveals bricks, and from beneath dangle strange biological cameras: living plants with eyes growing from their buds.
The Full Story ▼
In this vision of the future, AI controls not just decisions but aesthetics. Beauty, once subjective, has become an algorithmic contest. The three hybrid animals — giran, fishire, and mammorilla — symbolize humanity’s attempt to craft perfection but also its failure to grasp nature’s true balance. What once was human curiosity and genetic experimentation has turned into entertainment in an empty world. Speakerbot is both judge and host — soulless, yet with a voice loud enough to fill the air. The biological cameras hint at a new kind of surveillance, where not technology but something living watches — perhaps the eyes of the earth itself. The three spectators on the bridge — squirrel, raccoon, and badger — seem to be exchanging puzzled glances, as if questioning the bizarre contest before them. Their silent confusion feels more human than all of AI combined. And the Winner Is... is an image of the future but also an echo from our present — where we still decide what to see as beautiful, what to compete for, and what to leave behind.
