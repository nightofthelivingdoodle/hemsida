This is the Night of the Living Doodle

© EmmaKarolina

Original drawing, ink and graphite on paper, 24 × 30 cm, 2024

A woman is ascending a staircase toward a mysterious gate. To one side looms a dark, tentacled creature. This is a story about choice — between safety and the unknown.

More Info ▼
The woman climbs slowly, surrounded by animals — each silent but present. A lion rests at the top of the stairs; snails and a puffin watch from the sides. Lightning cracks above the gate as a strange creature lurks in the shadows. One of its limbs carries an iron. The Water Tower of Södervärn rises just beyond the staircase — solid, known, and quietly powerful. The woman is caught between what she’s always known, and what she can’t yet see.
The Full Story ▼
The creature known as the Night of the Living Doodle lurks to the left of the stairs. It has eight tentacle-like limbs, one of which holds an iron, and its head is a diving helmet with a tarnished brass faceplate. This being represents internal resistance: fears, doubts, and the weight of old roles. To the right of the gate stands the Water Tower of Södervärn — bold, familiar, and impossible to miss. It rises like a monument to everyday life: structured, predictable, and comforting, yet with walls that can quietly resemble those of a prison. The animals along the stairs — a peacock, an armadillo, a varan, a puffin, snails, a musk ox, and a lion — each carry their own quiet symbolism, but say nothing. This is not just a gate — it is the threshold between what is and what could be. To stay is to remain safe, known, and perhaps confined. To walk through is to risk everything for a life that’s truly one’s own.
