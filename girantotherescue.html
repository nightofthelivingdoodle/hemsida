The Full Story ▼
In a tightly packed prison yard, a naked woman stands burdened, carrying two heavy buckets. She represents a figure who lacks the strength to properly care for the neglected child lying curled in the scoop of a nearby tractor, turned slightly away from her. The yard is filled with symbolic creatures: a pelican perched on the tractor’s roof, a vulture’s head peering over the walls, a cautious cat moving quietly through the debris, and a curled armadillo resting on the ground. Missiles lean silently against a gate, marking boundaries without aggression. Above, the mythical Giran flies in—a fantastical creature symbolizing the idea that rescue must come from oneself, as no external savior truly exists. The background features the Edinburgh Airport Tower, standing tall as a symbol of society’s watchful gaze. Additional details such as a foxglove flower, a telephone, and a radiation warning sign evoke a toxic, hazardous environment. Scattered in the scene are landmines, where the artist’s signature is subtly embedded. The entire artwork layers themes of confinement, emotional neglect, vigilance, and the difficult path to self-liberation.
